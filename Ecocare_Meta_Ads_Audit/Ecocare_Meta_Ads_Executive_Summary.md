## The one-paragraph verdict

Ecocare's account is **healthy and holding, not collapsing.** In the last 30 days it produced **596 Instant-Form leads (+5.5%)** on **CA$17,863 spend (+16.7%)** at a **CA$29.97 CPL (+10.7%)**. The CPL rise is **entirely a media-cost story** — **CPM jumped +33.9%** while every funnel metric *improved* (CTR +14%, click-to-lead 11.0%→11.8%, leads-per-1,000-impressions +21%). The scary-looking recent 7-day dip (81 leads @ CA$33.27 vs 143 @ CA$30.39) is explained by **deliberate campaign pauses (WhatsApp, EV Charger ~17 Jul) plus a new Ottawa campaign in learning** — not a structural problem. Meta's own account **Opportunity Score is 53/100**, and its top fixes match ours.

## Top 5 findings

1. **Rising CPL = rising CPM from budget mix-shift.** Budget moved from the cheap water-softener "All In One" campaign (CPM ~CA$17) to the pricey "HVAC + Water Heater CBO – New" (CPM ~CA$76). Fix the *allocation*, not the funnel.
2. **The account leans on one ad.** The *Water Softener CA$29.99/mo* video drove **278 of 596 leads (47%)** and is fatiguing (CPL CA$19.97→25.46→30.53 over time; hook rate slipping). Refresh it — highest leverage, lowest risk.
3. **The cost-per-lead control did backfire — and is already gone.** A "Cost per result goal" (~CA$30 cap) on the old HVAC campaign starved delivery to ~half budget (CA$555 spend). The team rebuilt it on **Highest Volume** (now CA$6,312 / 185 leads). **No active campaign uses a cost cap today**, so the cap is **not** the current problem. *(Meta's change-log tool is unavailable for this account, so this is inferred from bid-strategy fields and delivery — and labelled as such.)*
4. **Optimisation-goal / tracking mismatch.** 6 of 13 ad sets optimise for **QUALITY_LEAD**, which needs a CRM quality signal — but the **GHL CRM pixel last fired Nov 2025** and **no custom conversions exist.** Tellingly, the two **LEAD_GENERATION** ad sets have the **cheapest CPLs (CA$23–25).**
5. **Structural fragmentation & waste.** 3 near-identical HVAC ad sets self-compete (Meta: −27% cost/result available); **Instream Video** placement wastes budget (CA$75 CPL); paused **WhatsApp** was 9% of spend for <2% of leads.

## What to do in the next 48 hours

- **Exclude Instream Video** (pure waste). **Keep bidding uncapped** (Highest Volume).
- **Don't touch** the winners: All In One and Tankless (a big edit resets learning).
- **Start 2–3 fresh water-softener creatives** (don't pause the original until replacements are live).
- **Confirm the CRM "Quoted" event** (100% email/phone match, currently unwired) and map it to a custom conversion.
- **Decide EV Charger** (efficient CA$20 CPL but non-core): re-activate at lower frequency or retire.
- **Reallocate paused-campaign budget** to All In One + Tankless in 20–25% steps.

## The path to more leads at a lower CPL

Refresh the water-softener creative → consolidate HVAC-New's 3 ad sets → cut Instream/WhatsApp waste → reconnect the CRM quality signal → then scale All In One and Tankless on Highest Volume. **Operational benchmark: CA$26–28 CPL at ≥600 leads/30 days** — an operational target from the account's own history, **not** a confirmed profitable CPL. The true allowable CPL must come from booked-rate × close-rate × customer value.

## The honest caveat

Meta reports **modelled, attributed Instant-Form submissions** — not booked jobs or revenue. **Lead quality cannot be confirmed from Meta data alone.** Until CRM/offline-conversion data is connected, we can identify **cheap** leads but not **qualified** ones. Connecting that loop is the single most valuable next step for turning "more leads" into "more customers."
