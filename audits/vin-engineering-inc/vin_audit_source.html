✦Executive summary

Vin Engineering's Google Ads account is a young, single-campaign account with genuinely good fundamentals that is being held back by a small number of fixable issues. In the last 30 days it spent CAD $1,472 and produced 13 leads (9 form fills + 4 calls) at a $113 cost-per-lead. Nothing here is "broken" — but roughly a third of every dollar is being taxed by a weak landing page, and about a quarter of available searches are missed because of it.

The single biggest lever: Google's own landing-page (post-click) Quality Score is Below Average on nearly every keyword. That low score is why the average click costs $7.44 (high for this niche) and why the campaign loses 43.8% of available impressions to Ad Rank. Fix the landing page and three things improve at once: lower CPCs, more impressions inside the same budget, and a better conversion rate — compounding into a materially lower cost-per-lead.

7Landing page experience — the #1 lever

Ad destination: vingcinc.com/offers/vingcin-landing.html

We could not crawl the page directly for this audit (the site blocks automated requests), but we don't need to guess — Google has already graded it for us. The Below-Average post-click Quality Score repeating across virtually every keyword (Step 5) is Google's landing-page verdict, and it is the root cause of the account's two most expensive symptoms: the $7.44 CPC and the 43.8% of impressions lost to rank.

Why this is the highest-ROI fix in the account.Landing-page experience feeds Quality Score → Quality Score sets your CPC and your Ad Rank → Ad Rank governs impression share and position. Improving the page moves all four at once: cheaper clicks, more impressions in the same budget, better positions, and a higher conversion rate. No other single change compounds like this.

Recommended landing-page priorities (standard high-intent local-service best practice)

  • Message-match the ad, section by section. The hero should repeat the exact promises the ads make — "Legal Basement Permit – $1,199", "drawings + permit + build, one team", "licensed engineers / BCIN-certified", "avoid fines", "unlock rental income". Congruence between ad and page is precisely what the post-click score measures.
  • Build dedicated pages per theme so "basement permit drawings" traffic and "legal basement / second unit" traffic each land on a page about that, not a generic offer. (Pairs with the ad-group split in Step 3.)
  • Above-the-fold conversion path: a click-to-call phone number (tel: link) and a short form (name, phone, postal code, brief message) visible without scrolling — mobile is 72% of your traffic.
  • Trust signals: BCIN / licensed-engineer credentials, Google review stars, number of basements legalized, service-area map, and a visible privacy link.
  • Speed & mobile: ensure fast mobile load and a responsive layout — both are inputs to the landing-page experience score.

Recommendation: a manual UX + PageSpeed review of the live page is the natural next step to convert this diagnosis into a specific fix list.

8Ad copy & creative

The single responsive search ad is a relative strength. Ad Strength is "Good," all 15 headlines and 4 descriptions are approved, and the copy is benefit-led and specific.

What's working in the copy.Strong, varied headlines — "Legal Basement Permit – $1199", "Licensed Engineers & Builders", "BCIN-certified Permit Designs", "Rent Your Basement Legally", "Avoid Fines, Build Legally", "Free Basement Assessment" — cover price, credibility, outcome and urgency. This is a well-constructed ad.

Where to push it further

  • Add a second RSA to each ad group so Google can test creative and your assets can graduate from "Pending" to graded faster.
  • Review the keyword-insertion headline "{KeyWord:Vin Engineering Inc.}" — confirm the inserted search terms always read cleanly; consider pinning a brand or offer headline to position 1 for consistency.
  • Add / confirm ad assets: sitelinks, callouts, structured snippets, a call asset and a lead-form asset all lift CTR and Ad Rank at no extra CPC — quick wins while the page is being rebuilt.
  • Tighten relevance once ad groups are split (Step 3): a "drawings" ad group should lead with drawings headlines, a "second-unit / rental" group with rental-income headlines.

10Bidding & budget

  • Strategy: Maximize Conversions with no Target CPA. This tells Google to spend the full budget for as many leads as possible with no efficiency ceiling — fine to start, but now that you have conversion history, add a Target CPA (begin near your current ~$113 and tighten as Quality Score improves) so bidding chases efficiency, not just volume.
  • Budget: $50/day is losing 22% of impressions to budget. Do not raise it yet — first fix the landing page and Quality Score so each dollar buys cheaper clicks, then scale into the rank headroom that opens up.
  • Optimization score 55.5%: review Google's recommendations tab and apply only the ones consistent with this audit (ignore broad-match / Display-expansion / Search-Partners suggestions, which would undo your clean setup).

✓What's working well

A credible audit is balanced. Several things here are done right and should be protected, not "fixed":

  • Clean network setup — Google Search only; no Search Partners or Display draining budget.
  • Correct local targeting type — "Presence," so you pay for people actually in your service area.
  • Disciplined match types — Phrase/Exact only, no broad-match spray.
  • Real conversion tracking — lead forms and calls, not vanity events.
  • Strong ad copy — "Good" Ad Strength, specific and benefit-led.
  • Healthy 6.21% CTR — your ads earn clicks; the constraints are downstream (page & Quality Score).

→30 / 60 / 90-day action plan

First 30 days — stop the leaks, verify the foundation

  • Import the negative-keyword CSV; remove Cambridge (UK); prune the stray/off-core keywords.
  • Verify conversion tracking (firing, dedup, the double-counted calls) and mark the true primary action.
  • Add a Target CPA (~$113) to the bidding strategy.
  • Brief and begin the landing-page rebuild.

Days 30–60 — fix the foundation, lift Quality Score

  • Ship the rebuilt, message-matched landing page(s).
  • Split into 3–4 themed ad groups; add a 2nd RSA and all ad assets per group.
  • Import qualified-lead / booked-job conversions from the CRM.

Days 60–90 — scale what works

  • Re-measure Quality Score, CPC and impression share; expect cheaper clicks and higher impression share.
  • Tighten Target CPA; scale budget into the reclaimed rank/budget headroom.
  • Expand into proven themes (e.g. second-unit/rental, basement drawings) and top cities (Toronto, Mississauga).
Realistic expectation — and what we won't over-promise.Fixing the landing page and Quality Score should lower your cost-per-click and raise impression share, which together should bring the cost-per-lead down over 60–90 days while increasing lead volume within the same budget. We can't guarantee a specific number: this is a young, low-volume account, results depend on the rebuilt page and on your close rate, and Google's auction is outside anyone's control. What we can commit to is removing the waste, fixing the root cause, and measuring honestly.
